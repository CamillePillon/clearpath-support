Foundations

Publishing Essentials

Effective publishing is more than making content available — it is a structured process that ensures accuracy, credibility, and long-term usability.

Governance & Workflow

  • Define clear roles for drafting, reviewing, approving, and disseminating content
  • Maintain version control and documented editorial standards
  • Ensure authorship attribution and transparent approval pathways

Quality Assurance

  • Verify technical accuracy and internal consistency throughout
  • Apply peer review proportionate to the document's purpose and impact
  • Structured review procedures are essential for normative or policy documents

Audience Orientation

  • Define user groups before deciding on tone, structure, and format
  • Match the format to the need: report, summary, toolkit, or digital resource
  • Build in accessibility standards from the outset — not as an afterthought

Content Lifecycle

  • Publishing is not a one-time event — plan for updates, archiving, and retirement
  • Outdated materials erode trust; maintenance mechanisms are part of the process

Complexity & Care

Multilingual Publishing

Publishing across languages introduces specific challenges that go beyond translation. Managing them well requires planning, coordination, and clear governance.

Translation as Adaptation

  • Carry meaning and nuance across linguistic and cultural boundaries
  • Rigorous terminology management for technical domains
  • Adjust cultural references and idioms for specific audiences

Global Consistency

  • Use harmonised glossaries and translation memories
  • Establish authoritative language versions for discrepancies
  • Systematic updates across all versions when source text changes

Design & Layout

  • Account for text expansion and contraction across languages
  • Plan for right-to-left scripts and font compatibility early

Workflow Integration

  • Embed translation early in the production timeline — not as a final step
  • Coordinate authors, editors, translators, and reviewers with clear timelines

Opportunity & Governance

Generative AI in Publishing

Generative AI tools are reshaping the publishing workflow. Used thoughtfully, they can increase efficiency — but they require clear governance and critical oversight.

Opportunity

What AI Can Help With

  • Early drafting and ideation support
  • Summarising and restructuring existing content
  • Supporting language standardisation and translation
  • Automating repetitive formatting tasks
Risk

What Requires Careful Management

  • Countering inaccuracies or fabricated references
  • Human review remains indispensable for editorial judgment
  • Protecting sensitive information from external systems
  • Clarifying copyright and authorship attribution
Responsible Integration

How to Use AI Well

  • Define clear organisational usage policies
  • Require full disclosure of AI use
  • Apply critical review to all AI-assisted outputs
  • Integrate into established processes, not as a bypass
